# 2027 Jade Trend Research Design

**Goal:** Design a research system that can answer "what jade products should we build next year?" with evidence from Xiaohongshu content, product images, comments, and adjacent category signals.

**Primary outcome:** A 2027 jade trend map focused on product and design direction.

**Secondary outcomes:** A prioritized product list and design briefs for the most promising directions.

---

## 1. Decision Context

This research is not a general content report. It is meant to support product and design decisions for next year.

Current business context:

- Current core customers skew toward women aged 30-45 with a light-luxury and quality-consumption mindset.
- Growth ambitions include women aged 25-35 and younger style-forward consumers.
- The business intends to develop multiple product categories rather than a single hero item.
- Strategic interest areas include `agarwood + jade`, `dopamine color palettes`, and `18K gold`.
- The main price band is `3000-8000 RMB`, with some layering above and below.

This means the research must distinguish between:

- directions that work for the current customer base,
- directions that can unlock younger growth,
- directions that look visually fresh but may not fit the target price band or buyer psychology.

## 2. Research Question

The core question is:

> Within the next 12 months, which jade design languages are most likely to create demand in the 3000-8000 RMB range, while remaining credible to the current 30-45 customer base and attractive to younger growth segments?

Supporting questions:

1. Which product forms, colors, material combinations, and styling systems already perform well in the jade category?
2. Which adjacent-category signals are likely to migrate into jade next year?
3. Which of those signals are merely aesthetic noise, and which are supported by interaction, comments, and repeat patterns?

## 3. Approach Options

### Option A: Jade-only market scan

Study only jade accounts, jade keywords, jade product posts, and jade comments.

Pros:

- Fastest setup
- Highest category relevance
- Easier to translate into product choices

Cons:

- Risks inheriting current category blind spots
- Weak at identifying future-facing style shifts
- Poor fit for testing `agarwood + jade` and `dopamine color` ideas

### Option B: Cross-category-first trend hunting

Use jewelry, fashion accessories, pearls, colored gemstones, and agarwood as the main input. Use jade only as a validation layer.

Pros:

- Stronger at finding new style language
- Better for younger-audience trend signals
- Good for hybrid concepts

Cons:

- Higher risk of fashionable but commercially weak conclusions
- Harder to map directly to jade sell-through
- More analyst judgment required

### Option C: Layered research model

Use jade as the trunk and cross-category signals as a growth layer. Build the view in four levels:

1. Jade category reality
2. Product-level image and content decomposition
3. Adjacent-category migration signals
4. Weighted trend judgment

Pros:

- Best balance between relevance and future signal capture
- Strong fit for both current business and growth ideas
- Reduces the risk of chasing visually attractive but commercially weak concepts

Cons:

- More setup work
- Requires a clearer tagging and scoring system

### Recommendation

Use **Option C**.

This is the best fit because the team does not need a style moodboard. It needs a decision system for choosing what to build. Option C lets us ground conclusions in jade category evidence while still leaving room for signals like `18K gold`, `color play`, and `agarwood + jade`.

## 4. Study Scope

### Time window

- Primary observation window: last `6-12 months`
- Short-term trend check: last `90 days`

This allows us to separate:

- stable demand patterns,
- emerging but not yet saturated signals,
- short-lived spikes.

### Platforms

Primary platform:

- `Xiaohongshu`

Future expansion candidates:

- Douyin for commerce intensity
- Taobao/Tmall storefront observation for price packaging

This design focuses on Xiaohongshu first because it is the strongest environment for product imagery, taste formation, and early trend signal capture.

### Product scope

We should not constrain the study to one item type. The research should cover:

- necklaces / pendants
- earrings
- rings
- bracelets / bead bracelets
- mixed-wear styling systems

The analysis will still track item type as a core dimension so we can see where each trend is strongest.

## 5. Data Collection Design

The collection layer should be built from three pools, each with a different purpose.

### Pool A: Core jade account pool

Purpose:

- Understand the category baseline
- Identify current winning product patterns
- Find recurring design systems rather than one-off virality

Target size:

- `30-50` jade-related Xiaohongshu accounts

Account types:

1. Commercial conversion accounts
2. Aesthetic/editorial jade accounts
3. Younger experimental or style-led accounts

Selection criteria:

- Frequent product-focused posting
- Consistent interaction on product notes
- Clear visual identity
- Visible selling intent or strong product orientation
- Recent activity within the observation window

For each account, collect:

- profile metadata
- recent `6-12 months` of notes
- note-level interaction fields
- note images / covers
- comments for high-value notes

### Pool B: Keyword result pool

Purpose:

- Escape account-selection bias
- Capture small creators, new formats, and breakout posts
- Discover trend language before it is concentrated in a few accounts

Keyword groups:

1. Jade basics
2. Modernized jade design
3. Material-combination ideas
4. Style and occasion language
5. Price and gifting language

Seed examples:

- `翡翠吊坠`
- `翡翠耳饰`
- `18k金翡翠`
- `翡翠叠戴`
- `翡翠项链设计`
- `沉香 翡翠`
- `东方轻珠宝`
- `多巴胺 珠宝`
- `彩色宝石 项链`
- `送礼 珠宝`

For each keyword:

- collect top and recent results
- capture note metadata and cover image
- preserve result ranking context where possible

### Pool C: Adjacent category pool

Purpose:

- Find design signals likely to migrate into jade
- Test the commercial relevance of `18K gold`, color play, mixed materials, and Eastern-modern hybrids

Target size:

- `20-30` accounts across adjacent categories

Priority categories:

- 18K gold light jewelry
- pearls
- colored gemstones
- agarwood / Eastern accessory accounts
- fashion accessories with strong styling identity

For these accounts, the goal is not to count sales but to identify rising visual and product language.

## 6. Unit of Analysis

The study should not treat the account as the final unit. The main unit should be the **product note** and, when needed, the **product motif** inside the note.

Why:

- One account may contain several distinct product strategies.
- One note may show multiple products or a styling system.
- Trends often emerge at the level of shape, color, material pairing, and presentation style, not at the creator level.

Recommended hierarchy:

1. Account
2. Note
3. Product motif
4. Trend cluster

## 7. Analysis Framework

Every high-value note should be tagged through a common framework so we can compare across accounts and categories.

### Dimension 1: Product form

- necklace / pendant
- earrings
- ring
- bracelet / bead bracelet
- set / layered styling

### Dimension 2: Jade material expression

- color family
- transparency level
- saturation
- size impression
- polished / carved / bead / geometric form

### Dimension 3: Material combination

- jade only
- jade + 18K gold
- jade + diamonds / zircon / stones
- jade + agarwood
- jade + pearls
- jade + mixed color stones

### Dimension 4: Shape language

- donut / ring
- teardrop
- cabochon / egg shape
- square / plaque
- bead / strand
- irregular / organic
- modular / stacked / convertible

### Dimension 5: Metal and craft language

- fine delicate metal
- bold chain
- starburst / sculptural detailing
- vintage detail
- minimalist setting
- playful or unconventional hardware

### Dimension 6: Styling and scene

- office / daily wear
- gifting
- festival / occasion
- Eastern styling
- fashion layering
- collectible / special-piece framing

### Dimension 7: Visual system

- background tone
- photography distance
- hand / neck / ear live wear versus isolated product shot
- light softness / reflectivity
- palette contrast

### Dimension 8: Narrative strategy

- scarcity
- craftsmanship
- taste / style identity
- emotional desire
- gifting
- symbolism / blessing / meaning

### Dimension 9: Audience fit

- current core audience fit
- younger growth fit
- price-band credibility

## 8. Image-first Analysis Rule

This research must not infer product demand from text alone.

Image review is mandatory for all high-value samples because key sell-through signals often sit in:

- the color relationship between jade and metal,
- the scale and proportion of the piece,
- whether the product feels traditional or modern,
- how premium the finish appears,
- whether the styling invites self-wear, gifting, or collecting.

Operational rule:

- For each selected high-value note, review the main product image or cover before coding conclusions.
- If the image cannot be accessed or interpreted clearly, mark the note as incomplete rather than forcing a conclusion.

## 9. Scoring Model

Trend conclusions should come from weighted evidence, not intuition alone.

### Evidence buckets

1. Image strength
- Visual distinctiveness
- Modernity versus datedness
- Material harmony
- Memorability

2. Interaction strength
- likes
- saves / collections
- comments
- shares
- total interaction
- save-to-like and comment-to-like patterns when available

3. Comment signal strength
- purchase intent
- fit questions
- material questions
- styling desire
- gifting intent
- repeat praise patterns

4. Cross-category resonance
- whether a similar aesthetic signal is also rising in adjacent categories
- whether the signal appears in multiple unrelated accounts

### Suggested scoring logic

Use a 5-point scale for each bucket:

- `1` weak
- `2` early / scattered
- `3` moderate
- `4` strong
- `5` very strong

Suggested weights:

- Image strength: `30%`
- Interaction strength: `25%`
- Comment signal strength: `20%`
- Cross-category resonance: `25%`

This weighting reflects the study goal: identify future-facing product directions, not simply content winners.

## 10. Output Structure

The final research output should contain three layers.

### Layer A: 2027 jade trend map

For each trend, include:

- name
- short description
- why it matters
- supporting evidence
- audience fit
- price-band fit
- confidence level

Trend buckets should include:

- confirmed continuations
- emerging opportunities
- watchlist experiments
- avoid / low-fit directions

### Layer B: Product priority list

Translate the trend map into:

- build now
- test in capsule runs
- hold / monitor
- avoid for current strategy

### Layer C: Design briefs

For each priority direction:

- target audience
- product category
- material mix
- color system
- metal language
- key silhouette
- emotional hook
- content presentation cues

## 11. Special Focus Topics

The research should explicitly test these three themes rather than treating them as side notes:

### Theme A: `Agarwood + jade`

Questions:

- Does it read premium, cultural, and modern at the same time?
- Which form factors make it wearable versus old-fashioned?
- Is the demand stronger in bracelets, pendants, or styling sets?

### Theme B: Dopamine color in jade-related jewelry

Questions:

- Which kinds of color contrast still feel premium?
- How much color play can the 30-45 audience accept?
- Can color be carried by side stones, enamel, cord, or mixed materials rather than jade itself?

### Theme C: `18K gold` modern jade language

Questions:

- Is the winning direction delicate luxury, sculptural modernity, or bold chain styling?
- Which shapes feel most compatible with 18K gold in the target price band?
- Which executions feel elevated versus mass-market?

## 12. Risks and Controls

### Risk 1: Popularity ≠ product demand

Control:

- Use comments and product-image analysis, not only likes.

### Risk 2: Account bias

Control:

- Combine account pool and keyword pool.

### Risk 3: Style signal without commercial fit

Control:

- Score price-band credibility and audience fit explicitly.

### Risk 4: Over-reading one-off novelty

Control:

- Require repeat evidence across multiple notes or categories for strong conclusions.

## 13. Success Criteria

This study is successful if it can produce:

1. A trend map with evidence-backed trend clusters, not vague style adjectives.
2. A shortlist of product directions relevant to the `3000-8000 RMB` price band.
3. Clear differentiation between:
   - what works for the current 30-45 audience,
   - what can attract 25-35 and younger buyers,
   - what should remain experimental.
4. Actionable design briefs that a product team can use immediately.

## 14. Recommended Next Step

Build the execution plan around four phases:

1. sample design and source list creation
2. data collection and normalization
3. manual coding and image review
4. trend synthesis and product recommendation output
